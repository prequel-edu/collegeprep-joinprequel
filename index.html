The Curriculum

12 weeks of foundations. 36 weeks of building.

Every week has a focus. Every focus has a deliverable. By month 12, your teen has a real portfolio and a college plan to match.

  • Week 1: What admissions officers actually want, and what the Masterpiece looks like in their eyes.
  • Week 2: Finding a real problem worth a year of your teen's life.
  • Week 3: First user interviews. Talking to people, not surveys.
  • Week 4: Validating the idea with 5 real people who would pay or use it.
  • Week 5: First small build. The "ugly version" that proves the concept.
  • Week 6: Tools and AI tooling overview. What to use, when.
  • Week 7: Build and launch the MVP. Real users hit it.
  • Week 8: First feedback loop. Iterating on what real users said.
  • Week 9: Distribution basics. Why "if you build it they come" is a lie.
  • Week 10: Storytelling. How to talk about what you're building.
  • Week 11: Picking the Masterpiece direction for the next 9 months.
  • Week 12: Foundations review. First demo day inside the cohort.
  • Months 4 to 6: Ship version 2. First press, first revenue, first 100 users or readers.
  • Months 7 to 9: Grow. Distribution, partnerships, or audience-building takes the front seat.
  • Months 10 to 12: Scale and document. Build the artifacts that make the college essay and interview impossible to argue with.
  • Throughout: live workshops, 1:1 coaching on demand, guest speakers, quarterly demo days with funding.
  • College roadmap with Austin Hughes runs in parallel from month 6 onward.
